And now the part that for luxury, in my opinion, is the whole game.

Governance.

Six weeks ago, a federal court ordered Perplexity's shopping agent to stop transacting on Amazon — the agent was disguising itself as a human shopper to make purchases. First major agentic-commerce ruling. The legal question the court answered: when an AI agent acts, who owns the action?

Trust Layer — 4 things stack

4 things the Salesforce Trust Layer does that other platforms don't.

Point to stack items · don't read · summarize

One. Action attribution. Every agent action ties to the human user who deployed it. When compliance asks "who did this," the answer is a name on your team.

Two. Dynamic grounding with sharing rules. Agents only see what the user is authorized to see. The CRM permission model extends to AI. No leakage across regions, brands, or VIP segments.

Three. Brand-voice control, toxicity detection, and prompt-injection defense — before the LLM call.

Four. Zero data retention with LLM partners. Your customer data does not train a third-party model. Ever.

And critically — guardrails are configurable by business users, not engineers. Your brand team and your compliance team shape agent behavior in natural language. No IT tickets.

The audit trails, the trust handshakes, the brand-voice scoring — none of it is something you bolt on later. That is the rail that makes the agent safe to put in front of a luxury customer.

High-confidence backstops
Is this realistic? Would real luxury shoppers actually plan with an agent?

And Williams-Sonoma's Olive runs the same occasion-based pattern in production today — 10,000 conversations a day, built in 60 days, 65% autonomous resolution. The pattern is real. Luxury context is the vocabulary.
How long to deploy?
Tony will speak to AI Retail Advisor specifics. Platform-side: Olive went live in 60 days at Williams-Sonoma. Storefront Next is a sprint, not a quarter. Implementation is configuration plus brand-voice tuning, not rebuild.
How does this work alongside our human clienteling team?
Augments, doesn't replace. The agent handles discovery, comparison, gift logic, post-purchase. The advisor gets the customer pre-warmed — full context, summarized intent, the open question that needs a human. Best practice: design the warm handoff first, the agent in isolation second.
How do we keep the agent on-brand?
2 layers. Template-bound execution — for high-stakes responses, the agent only selects from pre-approved content libraries. Brand-voice plus toxicity scoring before any response goes to the customer. Brand and legal configure this in natural language. No engineering tickets.
What about hallucination on materials, provenance, pricing?
That is exactly what grounding solves. The agent answers from your authoritative product catalog, your inventory system, your knowledge base. If the data is not there, the agent escalates rather than guesses.
Privacy — GDPR, CCPA, EU AI Act?
Built in. Trust layer masks PII before LLM calls, unmasks on return. Zero data retention with LLM partners. Article 50 transparency wired into the customer-facing flow. Brands configure, not architect.
What if the agent makes a mistake on a high-value customer?
2 answers. Guardrails plus circuit breakers — high-blast-radius actions require human approval. Audit trail — every agent action attributed to a human user. The model is not perfect. The governance is.
Can our existing data warehouse stay in place?
Yes. Zero-copy federation. Snowflake, Databricks, BigQuery — data stays where it lives. Data Cloud queries it in place. No migration project.
How does this compare to Sierra / Adobe / Shopify Sidekick / a standalone agent vendor?
(don't name competitors directly) The architectural choice is whether your agent is an extension of the systems your customer, product, and order data already live in — or a separate system you have to integrate, govern, and trust independently. Salesforce treats agents as users. They inherit the permissions, the data, the audit trails. At luxury scale, that difference is the whole game.
We use commercetools / Shopify Plus / a custom storefront — does this still work?
Defer to Tony — Merkle has done these integrations. Honest answer: deepest integration is Salesforce-on-Salesforce, where the trust-layer story is strongest. The agent layer is reachable from non-SF storefronts via APIs and the MCP layer. You don't have to migrate to start.
Multi-brand luxury house — LVMH, Kering, Chalhoub. How does ONE trust layer span dozens of maisons with different brand voices?
Same trust layer, brand-voice profiles per maison. Sharing rules carve the data. Brand-voice scoring carves the language. Each maison owns its content library, its tone scoring threshold, its compliance reviewer. One platform, n brand consciences.
We run Adobe Commerce + AEM + Marketo. Why bother switching?
Agentforce + Trust Layer can sit alongside non-SF stacks via MCP — that's the wedge. Deepest integration is still SF-on-SF, where the trust-layer story is strongest. We don't push migration off the stage. We push the conversation toward whichever system carries the most governance risk. Room 270 to map your stack.
