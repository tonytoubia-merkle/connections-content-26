Governance is the whole game in luxury.

Every interaction reflects on a brand that's spent decades — sometimes centuries — building equity. The brand can't be talked over by a model that hallucinated a material, mispriced a piece, or said something off-tone.

Federal court · March 2026 · agentic-commerce precedent

Amazon won an injunction against Perplexity's shopping agent for impersonating human shoppers.

"The agent knows. It doesn't say."

Merkle · AI Retail Advisor architecture spec

"And now the part that for luxury, in my opinion, is the whole game. Governance. Six weeks ago, a federal court ordered Perplexity's shopping agent to stop transacting on Amazon — the agent was disguising itself as a human shopper to make purchases. First major agentic-commerce ruling. The legal question the court answered: when an AI agent acts, who owns the action? That is the new exposure. In luxury, the price of an ungoverned agent is not a refund — it's a one-of-one client, a flagship's reputation, a brand book you've spent decades protecting. Brands have to govern the brand AND the AI powering the brand. Tony's spec captures it in six words — the agent knows, it doesn't say. That is the difference between a brand that put AI in production safely, and one in court."
